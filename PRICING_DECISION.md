# Poker Logic Lab — Pricing Decision Memo

_Decided: July 2026. Revisit once you have real traffic + conversion data._

## The decision

**Launch with a one-time price, not a subscription.**

- **Price:** $24.99 one-time (Lifetime), framed as a **founding / launch price** that rises to **$29** after launch.
- **Positioning anchor:** compare against competitors' monthly cost, not a fake former price.
- **Revisit:** add an optional recurring "Pro" tier later _only if_ you build genuine ongoing content and can measure better-than-median retention.

## Why one-time (the reasoning)

**1. The category is saturated with expensive subscriptions, and there's real fatigue.**
- GTO Wizard: $26–$206/mo (Premium $44/mo is the popular tier); prices rose in 2026.
- PokerSnowie: ~$24/mo discounted up to $249.75/mo direct.
- DTO Poker: subscription only, ~$10–$33/mo.
- One-time / lifetime pricing lives in poker _courses_, and that model is well-liked ("buy once, own it, move on").
- Reviewers openly call the big subscriptions "overkill for recreational players."

A one-time price doesn't compete on that crowded, resented axis at all. It's a differentiator.

**2. The LTV math doesn't clearly favor a subscription, and the risk is high.**
- Median subscription-app churn is ~13–14%/month (RevenueCat); the median app replaces its whole subscriber base every 7–8 months, and barely 10% of monthly payers reach year 2.
- Education apps churn ~10.5%/month; the first renewal alone loses 15–40%.
- At $4.99/mo with median churn, gross LTV is only ~$30–35 (realistically ~$25–30 net) — roughly a wash with the $24.99 one-time, but with far more variance and a monthly content treadmill to fight churn.

**3. Solo-founder / portfolio reality.**
- Subscriptions add churn management, dunning (failed payments), and a per-product content treadmill. Heavy overhead when the goal is launching multiple products.

## The founding-price framing (how to market $24.99)

- **Frame it honestly as a launch/founding price** that will rise: _"Founding price: $24.99. Going to $29 after launch."_ This is true — $24.99 is below one month of the big tools — so you have real room to raise.
- **Make urgency specific and real**, then honor it: _"Founding price for the first 100 members"_ or _"$24.99 through [date], then $29."_ No perpetual "introductory" price (that's a dark pattern).
- **Do not fabricate a fake anchor** (no "~~$49~~ now $24.99" for a price never charged).
- **Lead with the competitor anchor instead** — it's real and stronger:
  > "$24.99, one time. Less than one month of GTO Wizard. Yours forever."
- **Reminder:** urgency amplifies demand, it doesn't create it. At launch with little traffic, the price-increase countdown does little. The real lever is getting eyeballs on the page. Use the founding framing, but don't lean on urgency until you have steady visitors.

## Price-point note

$24.99 is likely underpriced given competitors charge more than that _per month_. Test $29 (or even $24.99 → $29 as the founding raise). Low price can signal low value to serious players, so don't go cheaper.
